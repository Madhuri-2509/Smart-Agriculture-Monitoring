# Smart-Agriculture-Monitoring
Abstarct: Agriculture plays a major role in food production and economic development. Farmers and agricultural
organizations often face challenges in monitoring crop conditions, identifying drought risks, and understanding
how changing weather patterns affect agricultural productivity. Traditional monitoring methods mainly depend on
manual inspections and delayed reporting, which makes it difficult to identify crop stress at an early stage. This
project proposes the development of an AI-powered Smart Agriculture Monitoring and Crop Intelligence Platform
that uses satellite vegetation data and weather information to analyze agricultural conditions. The system will analyze
NDVI vegetation patterns, rainfall levels, and temperature trends to monitor crop health and identify drought-prone
regions [1]. The project will use MODIS NDVI satellite datasets and NOAA weather datasets to perform exploratory
data analysis, vegetation monitoring, drought analysis, and GIS-based visualizations. In addition, simple machine
learning models will be implemented to classify crop conditions and identify possible drought risks.
